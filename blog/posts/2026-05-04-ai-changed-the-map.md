title: "AI Didn't Move Us Forward—It Changed the Map"
date: 2026-05-04
description: "AI didn't just make us faster. It changed the map. And now we have to learn how to navigate it."

---

There's a lot of talk about AI accelerating development.

Faster code.
Faster testing.
Faster iteration.

But that assumes we're still moving along the same path.

I don't think we are.

---

A better way to think about it:

The player didn't move.

The environment did.

If you've worked in a game engine, you've seen this.

The character looks like it's moving forward—

but sometimes it's the world moving underneath them.

That's what this feels like.

---

Everything still looks familiar:

we make things
we build systems
we ship work

But something fundamental has shifted.

**We can now build faster than we can understand.**

So it feels like progress.

But we've lost a clear reference point.

---

Before, effort gave you orientation.

- You built something hard — you understood it.
- You scaled something — you felt where it strained.
- You shipped something risky — you knew why.

Now that effort is compressed.

You can move quickly —

without really knowing where you are.

---

That creates a strange effect:

We don't just move faster.

We lose clarity on:

- what "good" looks like
- where the boundaries are
- where the goal line actually is

That sounds like a problem.

But it's also where things get interesting.

---

Because when the map changes,

it doesn't just remove familiar paths—

it creates new ones.

New entry points.

New ways to build.

New ways to break things.

We're already seeing it:

- different approaches to validation
- new expectations around observability
- more focus on system behavior, not just implementation

Not because we planned it—

but because we have to adapt to where we are now.

---

There will be bad outcomes in this phase.

There will also be genuinely new approaches that weren't possible before.

That's what happens when the ground shifts.

---

AI didn't just make us faster.

It changed the map.

And now we have to learn how to navigate it.
